Phase 1: Making the Implicit Explicit

1 Coaching Call | ~6-8 hours independent work | Week 1

Module 1: Diagnostic + Framework Recognition

Establish baselines and help Ella see that the analysis she performs in her writing IS the same framework the exam tests in MCQ format.

Part 1: Annotation Task

Give Ella an unseen passage (~800 words). Ask her to annotate it as if preparing to write a Rhetorical Analysis essay. Time her. Do NOT tell her what to look for — let her natural instincts guide the annotation.

Part 2: MCQ Transfer Test

Give her 10 MCQs on the same passage she just annotated. Time her. Mix of reading and writing MCQs.

Part 3: Recognition Exercise

After scoring the MCQs, show Ella that every question maps to a move she already made in her annotation:

"Question 3 asks about the function of paragraph 4. You wrote in your annotation: 'this paragraph shifts to a counterargument.' That's the answer. You already DID the analysis — you just didn't recognize it in MCQ form."
"Question 7 asks 'which revision would best support the writer's line of reasoning.' Your annotation identified what the author NEEDS here. The MCQ is asking you to SUPPLY it."

Exit Criteria & Branching

  • If annotation shows full STRUCTURE → FUNCTION → EFFECT AND she recognizes the MCQ mapping: skip Module 2, go directly to Module 3
  • If annotation is content-level despite strong FRQ writing: she needs Module 2
  • If she can't distinguish reading from writing MCQs: Module 2 is critical

Module 2: The Reading/Writing MCQ Distinction

Ella scored 0/7 on Skill 4, 0/3 on Skill 8, and 2/8 on Skill 6. These aren't analytical failures — they're FORMAT failures.

Skill Targets

Skill 4: CLE Writing0% → 50%+
Skill 8: STL Writing0% → 50%+
Skill 6: REO Writing25% → 40%+
Reading/Writing identificationN/A → 90%+ accuracy

Step 1: Worked Example (Coaching Call #1, 30 min)

Take 6 MCQs from the same passage: 3 "reading" and 3 "writing." Walk through the key distinction:

  • Reading MCQ: Look at the PASSAGE for the answer. What did the author do?
  • Writing MCQ: Look at the ANSWER CHOICES for the answer. Which choice best serves the argument's needs?

Question stem cues:

  • Reading: "primarily serves to," "can best be described as," "the author's tone"
  • Writing: "which of the following, if added," "the writer wants to," "which revision would best"

Step 2: Guided Practice (~2 hours)

  • 3 passages with 5 MCQs each (mix of reading and writing)
  • Before answering, Ella labels each question as "Reading" or "Writing"
  • For writing MCQs: practice reading ALL four answer choices before selecting

Step 3: Independent Practice (~1.5 hours)

15 MCQs (equal reading/writing), timed at exam pace. No labeling scaffold. Full explain-your-reasoning.

Step 4: Assessment

10 writing-type MCQs ONLY (Skills 4/6/8). Single attempt. Timed. Explain-your-reasoning.

Exit Criteria

  • 50%+ on writing MCQs (from 0% baseline)
  • 90%+ accuracy labeling reading vs. writing MCQs
  • Reasoning references argument FUNCTION, not just content preference

Phase 2: Speed and Transfer

1 Coaching Call | ~8-10 hours independent work | Week 2

Module 3: Speed Training — Analysis at Exam Pace

Ella left 11 questions blank. At her 44% accuracy rate, finishing the exam would gain ~5 raw points — potentially a full AP score point. Speed is her single highest-leverage intervention.

Skill Targets

Questions completed34/45 (76%) → 45/45 (100%)
MCQ pace>1.5 min/q → 1 min/q
Accuracy under speedN/A → 45%+ at exam pace

Step 1: Worked Example

Model exam-pace reading of a full passage set:

  • Structural skim (2 min): First and last sentence of each paragraph. Know the skeleton.
  • Question-driven re-reading (7 min): Read each question, locate relevant paragraph, apply STRUCTURE → FUNCTION → EFFECT.
  • Two-pass strategy: Confident questions first (~30 sec). Flagged questions with remaining time (~60 sec).
  • Time checkpoints: Q15 by min 15. Q30 by min 30. Q45 by min 45.

Step 2: Guided Practice (~3 hours)

3 passage sets (9 MCQs each), progressively tighter timing:

  • Set 1: 12 minutes (80 sec/q) — develop structural skim habit
  • Set 2: 10 minutes (67 sec/q) — two-pass strategy
  • Set 3: 9 minutes (60 sec/q) — full exam pace

Step 3: Independent Practice (~2 hours)

2 full passage sets at exam pace. Self-timed. Self-scored. Error analysis: rushed reading vs. genuine skill gap vs. writing MCQ confusion.

Step 4: Assessment

18 MCQs (2 passage sets), strict 18-minute timer. Single attempt. Exam-length passages.

Exit Criteria

  • 18/18 answered (zero blanks — the most important metric)
  • 45%+ accuracy maintained under time pressure
  • Structural skim under 2 minutes per passage
  • Can articulate time management strategy in own words

Module 4: Argument Structure Under Speed

Apply STRUCTURE → FUNCTION → EFFECT to argument-organization questions (Skills 5/6) at exam pace.

Skill Targets

Skill 5: REO Reading20% → 50%+
Skill 6: REO Writing25% → 50%+
Topic 3.3: Line of Reasoning~0% → 60%+
Topic 5.2: Unity/Coherence~0% → 50%+

Step 1: Worked Example (Coaching Call #2, 30 min)

Use Ella's own Module 1 annotations to show she already makes the analytical moves:

"You wrote 'this paragraph introduces the counterargument' — that's STRUCTURE. 'So the author can dismiss it' — that's FUNCTION. 'Which makes the reader trust the author's fairness' — that's EFFECT. Now let's do it in under 60 seconds per question."

Step 2: Guided Practice (~3 hours)

  • 3 passages (~1000-1200 words): 2-min structural skim, then 5 Skill 5/6 MCQs answered from skim alone
  • Track: which questions REQUIRED detailed reading vs. answered from skim
  • Argument mapping speed test: reconstruct scrambled passage in <3 minutes

Steps 3-4: Independent Practice + Assessment

3 new passages at exam pace + 15 MCQs (10 Skills 5/6 + 5 cumulative). Single attempt.

Exit Criteria

  • 50%+ on Skills 5/6 MCQs (2.5x improvement from baseline)
  • Structural skims under 2 minutes per passage
  • Function-level language in reasoning, not content-level
  • 90%+ on cumulative Skill 1/3/7 questions (maintaining strengths)

Full Exam Simulation

45 MCQs (45 min) + 3 FRQs (2 hrs 15 min). Full exam conditions. Never-seen material. No retakes.

Post-Mortem (~2 hours)

  • Primary question: Did she finish all 45 MCQs?
  • For every incorrect MCQ: skill, topic, reading/writing type, error category (speed/comprehension/format/analytical gap)
  • FRQ: score against AP rubric, target sophistication point
  • Pattern comparison to Module 1 diagnostic and Module 4 checkpoint

Exit Criteria

  • 45/45 answered (zero blanks)
  • MCQ: 50%+ (23/45)
  • No skill below 30%; Writing MCQs (Skills 4/6/8): 40%+
  • FRQ: 5.0/6 maintained

Module 6: Targeted Repair + Final Simulation

Address the 2-3 most persistent weaknesses from Module 5, then confirm readiness.

Targeted Repair (~4 hours)

  • If writing MCQs still below 40%: Return to Module 2 worked examples with new passages
  • If speed still the issue: Return to Module 3 progressive timing drills
  • If Skill 5/6 still below 40%: Additional argument mapping exercises

Essay Tune-Up + Final Simulation

  • 1 essay on weakest FRQ type (timed, 40 min), targeting sophistication point
  • Full exam simulation (45 MCQs + 3 FRQs), different material
  • Comprehensive post-mortem comparing to Module 5

Phase 3 Exit Criteria

  • MCQ: 55%+ on final simulation
  • 45/45 answered (confirmed)
  • No skill below 40%; Writing MCQs: 50%+
  • FRQ: 5.0/6 maintained, sophistication point on 1+ essay
  • Transfer gap < 15 points
