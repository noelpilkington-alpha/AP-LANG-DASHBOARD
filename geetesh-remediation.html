Phase 1: Building the Reading Foundation

1 Coaching Call | ~10-12 hours independent work | Week 1

Module 1: Diagnostic

Establish baselines across comprehension, analysis, and speed. Determine how much of Geetesh's exam failure is comprehension vs. analysis.

Part 1: Comprehension Baseline

  • Read unseen ~800 word passage (timed): summarize, identify thesis, name paragraph topics
  • Repeat with ~1200 word exam-level passage. Compare: does comprehension drop with length?

Part 2: Analytical Baseline

Using the 800-word passage: describe paragraph FUNCTIONS (not content), identify evidence + explain WHY author chose it, identify style choice + EFFECT, identify clause types (grammar baseline).

Part 3: MCQ Baseline

10 MCQs on the 800-word passage. Timed at exam pace. Mix of skills and reading/writing types.

Module 2: Strategic Reading

Geetesh-Specific Module: This module has NO equivalent in the other remediation courses. Geetesh's Grade 8.3 reading level means passage comprehension is the barrier. He can analyze what he can comprehend — but exam passages exceed his comfortable reading level.

Skill Targets

Passage comprehension (1200 words)TBD → 80%+
Structural skim time (1200 words)TBD → < 4 minutes
Thesis identificationTBD → 90%+ accuracy

Step 1: Worked Example (Coaching Call #1, 45 min)

Model strategic reading on an exam-length passage:

  • First/last sentence method (1 min): Read the first and last sentence of EVERY paragraph. These are where authors state their points and transition.
  • Skeleton map (30 sec): From first/last sentences alone, sketch the argument: P1: Problem setup. P2: Thesis. P3-4: Evidence. P5: Counterargument. P6: Rebuttal. P7: Broader implication.
  • Thesis confirmation (30 sec): Re-read the thesis paragraph fully to confirm.
"I've spent 2 minutes. I haven't read most of the passage in detail. But I know the thesis, paragraph functions, where the counterargument is, and how the conclusion relates. This skeleton answers ~60% of MCQs."

Step 2: Guided Practice (~3 hours)

5 passages of increasing length and complexity:

  • Passage A: ~600 words | Passage B: ~800 words | Passage C: ~900 words | Passage D: ~1000 words | Passage E: ~1200 words
  • Each: skim + comprehension check + 3-5 MCQs. Track skim time and accuracy.

Steps 3-4: Independent Practice + Assessment

3 exam-length passages: strategic skim (max 4 min), write thesis/functions/argument type, 5 MCQs each. Then 3 new passages timed at exam pace (15 MCQs total).

Exit Criteria

  • Thesis + paragraph functions of a 1200-word passage in under 4 minutes
  • 80%+ on comprehension checks
  • 50%+ on Skill 1 MCQs (from 40%)
  • If NOT met: repeat with new passages before proceeding

Module 3: How Arguments Are Built (Worked Examples)

Trace argument structure: paragraph functions, inter-paragraph dependencies, methods of development. Same core as Sara Beth's Module 2, but always begins with comprehension check and strategic skim.

Skill Targets

Skill 5: REO Reading40% → 55%+
Topics 3.1, 3.3, 5.2~0% → 50%+

Activities

  • Step 0: Comprehension check on every passage before analysis begins
  • Worked Example: Walk through ~800 word passage naming every paragraph's JOB, method of development, transitional logic. Draw argument map.
  • Guided Practice: 3 passages with decreasing scaffolding (thesis given → nothing given)
  • Independent Practice: 3 passages (~800-1000 words), full argument maps
  • Assessment: 10 Skill 5 MCQs, timed, single attempt, explain-your-reasoning

Exit Criteria

  • 55%+ on Skill 5 assessment (from 40%)
  • Argument maps use function-level language, not content-level
  • Comprehension check passes on every passage

Module 5: Evidence, Audience, and Claims

Apply STRUCTURE → FUNCTION → EFFECT to evidence/audience: WHAT evidence, HOW it supports the claim for THIS audience, WHY it's strategic.

Skill Targets

Skill 3: CLE Reading20% → 50%+
Skill 4: CLE Writing43% → 50%+
Course: Relating to Audience62.5% → 80%+
Course: Source Materials62.5% → 80%+

Activities

  • Worked Example (Coaching Call #2): Model evidence analysis — "The author uses a GOVERNMENT STATISTIC (structure) to GROUND an emotional argument in data (function) because the audience is POLICY SKEPTICS (effect)."
  • Audience comparison: 2 passages on same topic for different audiences
  • Source evaluation: 6 sources for Synthesis Essay simulation
  • Modes of persuasion drill: 5 short passages, identify appeal, cite evidence, explain
  • Assessment: 15 MCQs (10 Skills 3/4 + 5 cumulative Skill 5/6)

Exit Criteria

  • 50%+ on Skills 3/4 AND 55%+ maintained on Skills 5/6
  • Evidence analysis uses all three levels (structure, function, effect)
  • Describes audiences by values/beliefs, not demographics

Module 9: Targeted Repair + Final Simulation

Targeted Repair (~4 hours)

  • If Skill 3 still below 40%: Return to Module 5 evidence/audience worked examples
  • If Skill 6 still below 30%: Return to Module 4 writing MCQ worked examples
  • If comprehension still bottleneck: Return to Module 2 strategic reading with new passages

Final Simulation + Exam Strategy

  • Full exam, different material. Comprehensive post-mortem.
  • MCQ: Skim structure first, then targeted re-reading. Budget extra time for reading.
  • FRQ order: Start with Rhetorical Analysis (strongest), then Argument, then Synthesis (needs most source reading time).
  • Sophistication: Concession + rebuttal in every essay.

Phase 3 Exit Criteria

  • MCQ: 52%+ on final simulation
  • No skill below 30%; FRQ: 5.0/6 average
  • Sophistication point on 2/3+ essays
  • Transfer gap < 10 points

Unique Risk: The Reading Level Ceiling

Grade 8.3 reading level may impose a ceiling on improvement achievable in 5 weeks. Mitigations:
  • Strategic reading doesn't require higher reading level — it requires SMARTER reading
  • If comprehension plateaus: adjust targets downward for MCQ, focus more on FRQ (more processing time)
  • Track comprehension time and accuracy separately from analytical accuracy
  • Skill 8 (67%) shows he CAN answer complex MCQs when questions are self-contained
